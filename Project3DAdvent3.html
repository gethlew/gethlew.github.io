3D Advent Calendar

During the latter half of 2020 I decided that I wanted to improve my 3D modelling skills, therefore I decided to create a new 3D model each day up until Christmas. Throughout the three weeks it took for me to complete the models, I gained many skills which will help me throughout my 3D modelling journey. To see other 3D models I created please click on the “3D Models” tab above.

Day 13




Today the model built is based off the image on the right which is a Mitten/Glove.

This model was surprisingly difficult as I had to make the model look like fabric.

On my first attempt the model looked like plastic and too "perfect". I then decided to sculpt parts of the model to give it a more natural look. I also added hair physics to simulate fabric fibre.

Day 14




Today the model built is based off the image on the right which is a letter box.

For the scene, I decided to duplcate the cubes and rotate them and place them under the bottom of a tree to create a chritmas-like environmnet.

Day 15




The model today is based off the image on the right which is a wooden horse.

This one was a complex model to create. The legs of the horse were very basic modified cubes, and the roller board beneath the horse was created using a combination of cylinders. The saddle of the horse is my favourite part of this model. I feel it looks very good an the technique is interessting. The saddle is a cylinder which is scaled across two axis. I then removed parts of the cilynder to create the saddle shape.

Day 17




The model built is based off the image on the right which is a bell.

This is one of my favourite 3D models as it was simple to create, but also looks really good. I think the reason it stands out to me so much is the material used. The material makes the model look metalic. The placement of the bell on a tree also makes the model look very good as it helps visualize the model as something that could be seen in the real world.
